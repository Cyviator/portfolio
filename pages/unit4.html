Unit 4: Application of Threat Modelling and Management Techniques

Seminar 3 preparation - Workshop Activity

This artefact presents my preparation for the Unit 4 seminar on risk identification and modelling. It reviews Jbair et al. (2022) and examines how threat modelling supports risk assessment in cyber-physical systems by identifying key elements, interdependencies, and scenario-specific metrics used to prioritise vulnerabilities. The artefact includes my critical evaluation addressing several limitations that are not fully explored in Jbair et al.’s paper, such as its dependence on digital twins, limited scalability across CPS domains, and lack of alignment with established cybersecurity standards. This was done to assess the practical applicability of the proposed methodology beyond smart manufacturing and adds a more realistic understanding of how theoretical threat-modelling frameworks translate into real-world risk management practice.
