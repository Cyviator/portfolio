Unit 7: An Introduction to the Concepts of Quantitative Risk Modelling

Collaborative Learning Discussion 2 - Initial Post

This artefact presents my initial contribution to Collaborative Learning Discussion 2, based on Spring et al. (2021). I critically examined the limitations of the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS) and argued for more context-aware alternatives such as VULCON. My post evaluated how CVSS measures severity rather than risk and supported this critique with empirical evidence from recent studies.

Formative Activities - Risk Modelling

I completed the formative activity exercises (Winston, 2022; Downey, 2022), which helped me connect theoretical concepts with the practical application of risk modelling. To maintain and further develop my Python skills, I also replicated Winston’s Monte Carlo exercises (2022, Chapter 79) in Python, which strengthened my coding proficiency and enabled me to observe the impact of large-scale simulation iterations on the stability and accuracy of probabilistic outcomes, something typically limited in Excel. The Python simulation codes for the exercises are included as artefacts.
