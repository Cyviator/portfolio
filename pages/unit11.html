Individual Project: Executive Summary

This artefact presents my individual Executive Summary, which required a quantitative analysis of supply chain risks for a digitalising business and the design of a Disaster Recovery (DR) strategy to ensure continuity. I applied a Monte Carlo Simulation model to estimate probabilities of disruption to product quality and availability and designed a DR solution aligned with RPO and RTO targets of under one minute. I had prepared for this assignment through earlier activities that progressively developed the knowledge and skills required for this project:

  • Unit 7: Built technical competence in Monte Carlo simulations and Python through the formative risk modelling exercises.
  • Unit 10: Developed an understanding of RPO/RTO concepts and Disaster Recovery architectures through the seminar preparation activity.
  • Unit 11: Refined the ability to apply probability distributions within simulations through my Module Wiki contribution on Monte Carlo modelling.

Through this project, I deepened my understanding of quantitative probability modelling and the application of simulation-based approaches to real-world risk analysis. Although I encountered challenges in identifying reliable benchmark data and constructing a valid Monte Carlo function, this process significantly enhanced my ability to integrate statistical reasoning, technical implementation, and strategic decision-making within security and risk management.
