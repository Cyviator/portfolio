Seminar 5 Preparation - Workshop Activity

This artefact contains my preparatory work for the Seminar 5 activity. It includes my review of Aijaz and Nazir (2024), which analyses the modelling of Social Engineering Threats (SETs) using Attack Tree and Markov Chain models. I extended my response beyond the set questions by critically examining the study’s assumption that humans are the “weakest link” and by arguing for a more balanced socio-technical approach to risk modelling. During the seminar, I presented this critique, emphasising the need for risk models that integrate both human and technical dimensions. The discussion deepened my understanding of quantitative approaches to socio-technical risk and reinforced the importance of questioning dominant assumptions in security modelling.
