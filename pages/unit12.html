Unit 12: The Great Debate: What will be the most influential trend in SRM in the next 5 years?

Seminar 7 Preparation - The Great Debate: The Future of SRM

This artefact presents my contribution to the final seminar, The Great Debate - The Future of SRM. Throughout my research in this module, I discovered that many security risk management (SRM) methods, tools, and frameworks described in academic literature are rarely applicable in real-world contexts, often limited to specific industries or validated only in academic settings. Furthermore, these approaches frequently lack alignment with broader business strategies and organisational goals. My chosen trend, which I believe will be the most influential in SRM over the next five years, is the shift from a technical IT function to board-level accountability. My argument focused on how this transformation is being accelerated by regulatory frameworks such as the EU's NIS2 Directive and the aviation-specific PART-IS regulation. I proposed that this evolution will embed SRM within core business strategy, with executive oversight driving greater investment in methodologies, tools, and techniques to mitigate and solve security risks and their business impact more effectively.
